Round 3 · GOL

three letters, no tricks with arcs — the word itself does the work

3a

The Held O

GOL, but the O is stretched wide — one letter holding the whole scream. Says GOOOOL without adding a single character.

3b

The Ball

The O is just the ball — solid red, no outline, sitting dead center of the word. The simplest possible mark; unmistakable at any size.

3c

The Slice

Hard italic lean, red O, two clean speed cuts through the whole word — the ball moving too fast to read. Built for jersey chests and broadcast bugs.

3d

The Call

No devices at all — GOL! set tight in Anton, gold exclamation as the only accent. The commentator's word, shouted, on match red.

Round 2 · Blended G

the G built from the other half of the O's — one shared geometry, no glyphs, minimal gaps

2a

The Shared Ring

The G is the echo's first ring — same circle, same 13-unit stroke, wrapped nearly closed with its bar tucked in. A breath of a gap where it opens, then the echo pours out and the last arc lands the L. One geometry, zero borrowed letterforms.

2b

The Wrap

One outer circle, split at the top with no gap at all: its left half sweeps down as the G (bar tucking in at the lower right), its right half is the third O — exiting as the gold L. The original dot and two red arcs sit untouched inside. G and L literally share one O.

Echo Mark · G + L Iterations

the whole word GOOOL in one mark — G opens the echo, L rides out of the last arc

1a

The G Core

The G replaces the dot — the scream starts inside the letter. Two arcs echo out; the outer one drops a stem and runs a foot: the L. Reads GOOOL left to right in one gesture.

1b

The Ring G

No borrowed glyphs — the G is built from the echo's own inner ring (circle + bar, same 13-unit stroke). Pure geometry: every part of the mark is ring language. Most embroiderable.

1c

The Full Call

Keeps the original dot untouched — G stands before it, the three arcs are the OOO, and the last arc exits as the L. The complete GOOOL: letter, scream, letter.

1a reversed · red
